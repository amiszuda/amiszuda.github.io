abstract: V1216 Scorpii is a newly discovered SB2 eclipsing binary containing a Beta
  Cephei pulsator (Eze & Handler 2024). A first inspection of the light curve suggests
  a borderline between detached and semi-detached geometry and shows the O'Connell
  effect, high-amplitude pulsations, and a considerable asymmetry of the flux at ingress
  and egress of the primary eclipse. All these pose challenges to modelling the light
  curve of this system. Our analysis suggests that V1216 Sco is a post-mass-transfer
  binary, with 11.7 M⊙ and 4.3 M⊙ main-sequence stars. In our poster, we show the
  current state of the analysis of the system with the frequency search and the binary
  evolution modelling.
