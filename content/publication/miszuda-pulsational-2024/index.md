abstract: Binary systems, especially those that undergo eclipses, are well-proven
  benchmarks in testing stellar evolution theory. Precise stellar parameters that
  come from eclipsing binaries analysis aid systems' age determination along with
  tracing back their evolution history and settling the initial conditions that led
  the systems to their current form. Eclipsing binaries that contain pulsating components
  are a special subclass of binaries as they combine information coming from orbital
  and pulsational analyses. In recent years that subclass led to defining a newly
  formed branch of tidal asterosiesmology. Whereas the single-star pulsators are rather
  well understood, the precise effect that binarity and possible mass transfer introduce
  in the pulsational characteristics of components, especially those that gain mass
  during their evolution, is yet to be determined and systematised. In my talk, I
  will highlight some of the most important results from my studies of delta Scuti
  and beta Cephei pulsators in binary systems. In particular, I will discuss the systematic
  effects of mass accretion on the pulsational characteristics of stars, such as the
  evolution of radial mode frequencies during the main sequence evolutionary phase.
  Also, I will go into the effects that accumulation of helium in outer layers of
  accretor caused by mass transfer has on excitation of the high radial-order g modes
  in models of delta Scuti stars.
